History and Achievements

Founded in 1958 as Khabarovsk Automobile and Road Institute. Today TOGU is a modern university with rich traditions and innovative approaches to education.

Ranked among top-50 universities in Russia for education quality and research activity.

Historical Timeline

  • 1958 - Foundation of Khabarovsk Automobile and Road Institute
  • 1962 - Transformation into Khabarovsk Polytechnic Institute
  • 1992 - Status of technical university
  • 2005 - Receiving current name
  • 2023 - Ranked among top-50 Russian universities

About Khabarovsk City

Khabarovsk – capital of the Far Eastern Federal District, a modern metropolis with unique location at the confluence of Amur and Ussuri rivers. The city combines rich historical heritage with dynamic development, offering ideal environment for study and life.

Khabarovsk is known as 'city of bridges' – here is one of the longest bridges in Russia across Amur river. The city has developed infrastructure, many parks, embankments, modern shopping centers and cultural institutions.

Thanks to proximity to borders of China, Japan and Korea, Khabarovsk is an important international transport hub and center of intercultural interaction in Asia-Pacific region.

Language Support

TOGU provides comprehensive language support for international students, including:

  • Academic writing: Help with writing academic papers, essays and reports in Russian
  • Professional translation: Translator services for official documents and study materials
  • Conversation clubs: Weekly meetings with native speakers for practice
  • Language testing: Preparation for ТРКИ and other language tests
  • Digital resources: Access to online platforms, interactive courses and mobile applications

Our goal is to ensure complete language immersion and help students achieve level necessary for successful study and life in Russia.
